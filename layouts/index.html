BibleDocs

Scripture-centered writings for the Christian life

Overview Resources Contact

BibleDocs is a site dedicated to in-depth Bible teaching and resources related to Bible study, apologetics, evangelism, and more.

All Christians should read their Bible often, no matter what sort of role they occupy in the body of Christ. However, God has provided gifted individuals in the Church to supply Christians with additional spiritual nourishment (Ephesians 4:11-16). Pastor-teachers, for example, exercise their spiritual gifts to mine truth from scripture and present it in different forms to others, according to the ministries assigned to them by Christ (1 Corinthians 12:4-6). Bible teaching does not replace Bible reading; rather, it supplements it. Likewise, apologetics and evangelism helps never replace the Bible, but are used alongside it to make ministry more effective.

The materials on this site are written with this perspective in mind: that scripture is the final authority in all things, and that activities like Bible teaching, apologetics, and evangelism must necessarily stem directly from the Bible, being undertaken by gifted and prepared individuals.

Bible-centered

All materials on this site strive to keep the focus on the Biblical text. Greek and Hebrew are brought to bear as necessary, along with, for example, hermeneutics and textual criticism. Application (which depends upon individual circumstances) is clearly distinguished from actual Bible teaching.

Application-aware

Christians are called to learn Biblical truth, believe it, apply it in the real world under shot and shell, and then help others do likewise. While the specifics are always personal, this site structures content to help make application straightforward. Potential individual circumstances are often explicitly examined.

Level-appropriate

Different Christians are at different places in their spiritual walk. To make content useful to everyone, this site has both resources targeted at new believers and resources targeted at advanced Christians. All writing of both types also comes in two levels of technical complexity/prose difficulty.

BibleDocs has different sorts of resources suitable for different things.

At a high level, all Christians need to grow to spiritual maturity, pass tests of their faith until it is "battle hardened," and then come into their own ministries. This site puts an emphasis on materials that directly contribute to this progression. However, specialized materials are appropriate as well: those going into evangelism should be familiar with some of the common stumbling blocks in faith unbelievers face (such as blaming God for tragic events), those going into apologetics are going to need to know something of science and philosophy, those going into some form of Christian counseling and encouragement would do well to study how humans process emotions, and so on.

Types of resources offered

This site offers 10 different content types:

  1. Topical studies, similar to longform non-fiction books
  2. Shorter topical pages dedicated to brief but thorough treatments of individual concepts
  3. In-depth verse-by-verse studies, in the manner of an opinionated technical commentary
  4. More abbreviated notes on scripture, in the manner of a study Bible
  5. Questions and answers by topic (some questions come from correspondence with site readers, others are of my own creation; all answers are mine)
  6. Preparation and ministry information for specific ministries (e.g., apologetics, evangelism, music)
  7. Guides on various matters (e.g., using Bible study software)
  8. Recording archives of the meetings of a weekly Zoom Bible study group
  9. Resources relating to the study of the Greek language
  10. Resources relating to the study of the Hebrew language

These 10 categories of resources are organized independently. Since text is irreplaceable as a study medium, it is the dominant content type for this site.

Stay up to date

This site maintains RSS feeds for the main content streams to make it easy to stay up to date.

You can also stay up to date with site happenings by signing up for the BibleDocs mailing list. Here is a brief description of the list's mailing frequency and the sort of things that show up in the list's messages:

  • While I may occasionally send more or less emails (i.e., the frequency isn't strictly fixed), you can expect on the order of two emails a month from me. I won't spam you.
  • Messages typically involve news about the ministry (including, but certainly not limited to, descriptions of new content available on the site).
  • Messages may occasionally contain anonymized prayer requests that I view as particularly important to pass on. For example, "Please pray for Mark's father's battle with cancer."
  • I may also occasionally highlight material from other ministries that I find worthy of sharing. I am blessed to be in contact with a group of fellow teachers laboring in the truth, and will sometimes signal boost for them.
